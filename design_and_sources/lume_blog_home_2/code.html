The Soul of Space

By Elena Rossi March 12, 2024 schedule 8 min read

To understand the contemporary movement toward minimalism, one must look beyond the mere removal of objects. It is not an exercise in deprivation, but rather a deliberate curation of experience.

In the modern architectural landscape, the concept of "The Soul of Space" refers to the emotional connection a dweller has with their environment. This connection is often mediated by the tactile quality of natural materials—the grain of untreated oak, the coolness of honed stone, or the gentle irregularity of hand-applied plaster.

"Minimalism is not the lack of something. It is simply the perfect amount of something."
